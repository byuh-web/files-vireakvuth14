Quick Facts About Cambodia

Cambodia is a Southeast Asian country with a huge history, unique culture, and growing population. Its capital city, Phnom Penh, is the political and economic center of the country. The official language is Khmer, which is spoken by the majority of the population, and the national currency is the Cambodian riel, although U.S. dollars are also widely used. Cambodia has a population of over 17 million people, with most practicing Buddhism, which plays an important role in daily life and traditions. One of the country’s most famous landmarks is Angkor Wat, a historic temple complex that attracts millions of visitors each year. These basic facts provide a helpful overview of Cambodia’s identity and cultural background.
